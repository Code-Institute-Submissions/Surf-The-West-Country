1. Plage des Blanc-Sablons, Le Conquet

A good surf sport for beginners just a few miles away from the popular Brest. The beach is wide and sandy with plenty of room for busier days. It forms an easy wave in mid/high tides perfect if you are learning to surf. Be prepared for cold water tho! There is a surf school and a campsite located right next to the beach.

2. Fort-Bloqué, Guidel

A very consistent beach break nearby the river mouth. Under normal conditions, the waves break gently and it is best surfed at low tide. The surf here is good although summer tends to be very flat, which can be great for beginners who want to build up confidence out in the water. Watch out for rocks tho! There are some highly certified surf schools in the area which run daily surfing lessons and surf rentals too. La Plage du Loch is another fairly good surf spot for beginners/intermediates less than 3km north to Guidel. Car parking available

3. Sainte-Barbe, Plouharnel

Located on the extensive shoreline that runs between Kerhilio down to Penthievre, Les Crevettes is a fairly consistent beach break with nice small waves for learning to surf. It only works in high tide but it won’t get bigger than 2.5m-3m under normal conditions. The beach has no relevant hazardous but always keep an eye out for the current before you go in. Nearby beaches to the north like Sainte Barbe are often favoured for longboarders. The locals around here are very easy going and the spot is quite remote from the major surf spots in Quiberon which keeps the crowd away. Most surf schools are based in Plouharnel’s city centre and run classes in Les Crevettes. Car parking available.

4. Les Dunes, Vendee

An exposed beach break popular for its soft fun waves. The swell is never too big around here but it works best at mid/low tides when the offshore wind is northeast. Les Dunes is good for beginners and longboarders who like a nice mellow wall even though it can get flat during the summer months. The beach is located right in front of a camping site and a few metres away from the village which is ideal if you are planning to stay a couple of days. If you are an intermediate surfer looking for bigger waves you should check out the breaks further south to Vendee.

5. Les Conches, Vendee

This is probably one of the best surf spots in Vendee offering a hollow and powerful right under offshore winds from east northeast. The spot is very popular amongst locals due to its all-year-round surfable conditions besides getting better from April to September. It provides a really nice surfing session for intermediate surfers since it tends to receive a good size swell. There is one surf school located in the village of Les Conches that provides direct access to the beach and camping facilities.

6. Lacanau, Gironde

One of the best surfing spots in France and home to WSL Caraibos Lacanau Pro. Lacanau has an extensive 14km shoreline that offers amazing surf to all levels, although if the swell is big it can become quite rough, creating powerful and hollow waves suitable for more advanced surfers. Nonetheless, this is one of the best breaks to take surfing lessons in France due to the vast choice of surf schools and more experienced surfers that you can learn from. This place has a really nice beach vibe that comes alive in the Summer months and the surfing community is big around here. Plenty of campsites available.

7. Carcans, Gironde

Located a few km north to Lacanau, this break offers a very nice small wave that beginners find way much easier to ride than waves further down in the south. Additionally, this spot is always one of the less crowded in Gironde even during the summer months despite providing very enjoyable surfing sessions to all levels throughout the year. There are a bunch of surf schools and female-only surf camps to choose from in the lovely commune of Carcans as well as a camping site and a nearby lake.

8. L'Estacade, Capbreton

A stone’s throw away from the famous Hossegor beach! L’Estacade forms a waist high wave for beginners and intermediates, and it is sheltered from strong north winds due to the jetty located south of the port. Besides being a good beginners surf spot, it can become very crowded especially during the Summer.

9. Ondres Plage, Ondres

Another really good surf spot in Landes just a few miles away from Hossegor with consistent surfing all year round. Located in a long stretch of sand, Ondres Plage has plenty of space for everyone as it rarely gets crowded even on weekends! The wave is powerful and hollow with good barrels when the swell is big, however, most of the time you can find a gentle wave ideal for those who are learning to surf. Ondres works best in low/mid tides and offers a good selection of both left and rights. There are two surf schools located on the beach, a train station and a campsite just a few minutes away.

10. La Grande Plage, Biarritz

We finally got to the furthest south part of the West Coast! La Grande Plage is a memorable break in the history of Basque Coast surfing where you will find not only good waves but also a town revolving around surf. You probably won’t have to worry about surf schools and rentals as they happen to have in spades here. This spot offers a good medium hollow wave that works any time of the year despite the crowded line-ups. La Grande Plage is perhaps one of the best beach breaks suitable for beginners in the region of Biarritz as it rarely gets too big under normal conditions. The best offshore wind comes from the east.

11. La Côte des Basques, Biarritz

Home to the annual WSL Longboard Pro Biarritz contest and the birthplace of France’s surfing, this famous beach break has the perfect smooth wave for a fun session out in the water. The surf is best for beginners next to the headland where it is sheltered from north winds forming an easy mellow wall. It is usually better in low/mid tides when the offshore wind is southeasterly. Despite the vibrant atmosphere, it can get really packed with tourists and travelling surfers, especially during the summer months. As you would expect, there are plenty of highly certified surf schools and surf camps all over the shoreline.

12. Plage d'Hendaye

Another iconic beach in the French Basque Coast offering very good conditions throughout the year. It is a nice spot for beginner and intermediate surfers, short and longboards when the other breaks are closing-out. The waves tend to be good next to the south jetty where you will find a number of surf schools too. Crowded lineups and expensive car parking are a few downsides of surfing in Hendaye.
